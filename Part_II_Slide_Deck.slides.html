Part II - (Visualization Analysis of GoFordBike-System)¶

by (Emmanuel Olorunbogun)¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this presentation, the investigations were focused on characteristics of riders and how they performed in duration of rides in respect to the different:

  • User types
  • Gender
  • Hours of the day and
  • Days of the week.

Dataset Overview¶

After the dataset was cleaned in the exploratory part, its rows were reduced to 175147 and 8 columns. This columns includes:

  • bike_id
  • user_type
  • member_gender
  • bike_share_trip_for_all_trip
  • duration_min
  • Day_of_week
  • time
  • age

Key Insight 1: The Gender Proportion of Users in the GoFord Bike System¶

We have 3 different genders in the whole system.

  • The Male users are about 75% of the total users in the system.
  • The Females users are 23% of that population while the Other gender made up the remaining 2%.

Key Insight 2: What is the proportion of riders by user type?¶

  • The subscribers were about 90% of the total number of riders in the bike sharing system.
  • The customers made up the remaining 10%.

Key Insight 3: How many minutes does an average trip take?¶

  • Majority of the rides took about 5 to 12.5 minutes.
  • It can be said that most of the rides were over a short distance.

Key insight 4: What is the daily usage of bikes by user types?¶

  • Thursdays has the highest number of trips taken by both Customers and Subscribers.
  • Subscribers have higher usage of bike for every day of the week. This is expected, since the subscribers contribute about 90% of the total population of GoFord bike riders.

Key Insight 5: What is the daily average ride time by the user type?¶

  • For the total trips per day, the customers have a higher average ride time compared to the subscribers.
  • But both customers and subscribers had an increase in average ride time over the weekend (Saturdays and Sundays).

Key Insight 6: What time of the day is the busiest for riders?¶

  • 8AM and 5PM were the busiest hour of the day.
  • This might be a result of the resumption and closing time of riders from their various place of work.
